# Perfume Live Selling Playbook

This playbook is built from your current in-stock inventory in `data/whatnot.db`, with focus on the bottles that are deepest in stock and most likely to matter during live selling right now.

The goal is not to sound like a catalog. The goal is to sound like a confident perfume seller who can translate notes into something shoppers can feel immediately.

## How To Sell Perfume Live

### 1. Translate notes into a picture

Do not read `bergamot, jasmine, musk` and stop there.

Say what that means:

- Bergamot or citrus: clean sparkle, fresh opening, bright first impression
- Vanilla or tonka: creamy, cozy, sweet, comforting drydown
- Amber or benzoin: warm glow, softness, depth
- Musk: smooth skin scent, clean trail, soft finish
- Oud, patchouli, woods: richness, depth, dressy energy, confidence
- Fruits: playful, juicy, youthful opening
- Coffee, cacao, praline, caramel: edible warmth, dessert profile, cozy crowd-pleaser

### 2. Always answer four buyer questions

For every bottle, make sure your script answers:

1. What does it smell like?
2. Who would wear it?
3. When would they wear it?
4. Why buy this one instead of waiting?

### 3. Sell in plain language

Strong live wording:

- "This opens bright, then turns creamy."
- "This is the kind of scent that gets noticed in close conversation."
- "If you like sweet but you do not want sticky, this lands right in the middle."
- "This smells expensive without being hard to wear."

Weak live wording:

- "Top notes are..."
- "This is very nice."
- "This is long lasting" without saying what kind of wearer it fits

### 4. Use comparison lanes, not fake clones

Safer selling language:

- "designer-style lane"
- "luxury-feeling profile"
- "if you like warm sweet evening scents, this is in that lane"
- "if you like airy musky amber scents, this scratches that itch"

Avoid promising exact clone relationships unless you are sure.

### 5. Ask better chat questions

Instead of generic prompts, ask scent-based prompts:

- "Who wants soft vanilla over loud sugar?"
- "Are you a clean fresh person or a warm spicy person?"
- "Would you wear this to brunch, office, or date night?"
- "Who likes coffee in perfume when it stays smooth, not bitter?"

## Closing Guidance

When you sell, do not perform the whole note pyramid every time. Use this structure:

1. Open with the vibe.
2. Explain the smell in human language.
3. Name the wearer and occasion.
4. Give buyers a reason to act now.

Example:

> Chat, this is not a safe boring vanilla. This is creamy, warm, and dessert-like with a smooth musky drydown. If you love sweet fragrances for fall nights or cozy everyday wear, this is your lane. I have stock in hand right now, and this is one of those bottles people come back for after they finally try it.
